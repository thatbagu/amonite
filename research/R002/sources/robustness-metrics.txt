SOURCE: Qualities of Quality: A Tertiary Review of Software Quality Measurement Research
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2107.13687
Fetched: 2026-07-15

ABSTRACT:
This tertiary review synthesized 75 secondary analyses from an initial pool of 7,811 articles to examine how software quality is conceptualized and measured. The authors identify five distinct perspectives on quality: heuristic, maintainability, holistic, structural features, and dependability.

METHODOLOGY:
The research team conducted a three-stage review process, starting with a large dataset and filtering down to high-quality secondary analyses. This approach allowed them to synthesize patterns and trends across the software quality research landscape.

KEY FINDINGS:

Five Quality Perspectives Identified:
- Quality as heuristic judgment
- Quality as maintainability
- Quality as a holistic concept
- Quality as structural software features
- Quality as dependability

Three Critical Challenges:
1. Validity concerns: "widespread evidence of validity questions with common measures" undermines confidence in established metrics
2. Machine learning application gaps: The field applies ML methods "without adequate evaluation," risking unreliable predictions
3. Dataset obsolescence: Researchers rely on "aging datasets," limiting the applicability of findings to current development practices

PATH FORWARD:
The authors propose developing a theoretical framework grounded in developer experiences and real-world deployment contexts to address these weaknesses systematically.

MODULAR ROBUSTNESS TESTING FRAMEWORK:
A Modular Robustness Testing Framework is a compositional, extensible system designed to evaluate resilience in software, AI models, and cyber-physical systems. A well-designed modular framework supports reproducibility, cross-benchmark comparison, and rapid integration of new methods or targets.

REPRODUCIBILITY METRICS IN CONTAINERISED ENVIRONMENTS:

RELIABILITY-SPECIFIC METRICS IN USE:
Reliability-specific process and product-level metrics include: number of defects, defect rate, mean time to failure, mean time to repair, and transaction time.

SOFTWARE METRIC FRAMEWORK CRITICISM:
The field of software complexity metrics has faced criticism for the lack of testing, verification, and reproducibility of many metrics and case studies, with tools being developed to enable verification of metrics, reproducibility of experiments, and ease of implementation for new metrics.

SOURCE: Model Checking Guided Testing for Distributed Systems (Mocket)
URL: https://dl.acm.org/doi/10.1145/3552326.3587442 (EuroSys 2023)
Also: https://www.researchgate.net/publication/370601651_Model_Checking_Guided_Testing_for_Distributed_Systems
Fetched: 2026-07-15

KEY FINDING:
A technique fills the gap between formal specification and implementation in distributed systems by using the state space generated by formal model checking to guide testing for system implementations. The authors applied this technique to three popular distributed systems and found 3 previously unknown bugs.

The technique generates test cases from TLA+ models of distributed protocols. When applied to Raft, XRaft, and Zab protocols, this technique identified bugs in the implementations.

COMPARISON VALUE:
This paper provides a concrete methodology for quantifying the gap between spec-driven and implementation-level verification, and demonstrates that model-checking-guided approaches uncover defects missed by conventional testing.
